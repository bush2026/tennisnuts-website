The Story

Tournament Recap

History. The first-ever Tennisnuts Open — and it delivered on every front. An overwhelming response from players across the city, scorching May heat that tested every participant's resolve, and a draw with no age divisions meant the courts produced exactly what an Open should: unpredictable, emotionally charged, fiercely competitive tennis.

"Seniors against juniors on the same draw — and the experienced ones gave as good as they got. This is what the Open was built for."

Tanishq Jadhav has been here before. Clinching yet another Open Singles title — following his Flame tournament win — he came through opponents who each believed they had a shot, and proved again why he's one of the most dangerous players in the circuit. Aariz Khan pushed him hard and fully deserved his runner-up finish.

The Doubles final was a different kind of story. Shivam Padia and Sudhanshu Sawant, riding the momentum of the Wakad tournament win, showed they love a podium — and they love earning it. Aditya Bhave and Atul Karampurwala — veterans of the circuit — proving that experience isn't a handicap in Open tennis, it's a weapon. Senior doubles specialists everywhere will have taken heart from their run to the final.

Among the highlights: Sunil, Dr Ranade, Ajinkya, Joy, and Dr Ravi — senior players who stepped into an open draw and fought juniors point for point. These battles were some of the most watched of the day. The Open format creates exactly these collisions, and Pune's tennis community is richer for it.

Huge thanks to Sportsjam for powering the event. AWTA for providing excellent courts. Mahesh and Prakash for maintaining and watering throughout the day in that heat. Amit Kindo and Atul K for running the show on the ground. And a very special thanks to Amit Kindo for navigating the complex scheduling — making sure every player had back-to-back matches and the day stayed tight and competitive.
