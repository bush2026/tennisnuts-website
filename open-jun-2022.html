The Story

Tournament Recap

The Tennisnuts Open Amateurs Doubles — held on 11–12 June 2022 at AWTA CPR Pashan — drew an overwhelming response from players across the city. Backed by PMDTA and run with the rigour of a proper Open event, it proved beyond doubt that there was a real appetite for competitive open-format tennis in Pune. From the very first round, every match had something at stake — and the courts delivered.

What made this format special was the cross-generational mix in the draw. Senior amateurs — steady, experienced, tactically sharp — found themselves across the net from younger players who had only recently stepped away from their professional or college careers and entered business life. That collision of generations, of different styles and different stages of the game, produced the kind of tennis that you simply cannot manufacture with age-group restrictions. It was tennis in its truest open spirit.

Prasad Ingle and Rohan Phule took the title in commanding fashion — composed and clinical from the first round through the final, leaving no doubt that they were the better team on the day. The Kothari brothers — Dr Ravi and Dr Rahul — reached the final once again, their consistency and quality seemingly immune to format or occasion. A pairing that has become a fixture at the sharp end of every draw they enter.

"Good level of tennis — senior amateurs matching the game with youngsters who are done with their pro careers. That's exactly the spirit of the Open."

Heartfelt thanks to Venki and Amit for flawless scheduling and player communications — the event ran without a hitch because of their work. To Nilesh Sir, Aniket Wakankar Sir, and the CPR authorities for making the facility available and supporting the vision. A special mention of our YouTube live streaming pilot — we tried, we learned, we'll improve. Every first attempt teaches something, and this one taught us a great deal.
