📍 NCL Tennis Courts, Pune · The First Edition
The Beginning

No scoreboard. Just tennis.

The first Socials wasn't a tournament — it was a get-together. Round robin groups, everyone plays equal tennis, no one gets knocked out early. No results, just rallies and sandwiches.

The Story

Edition Recap

It started with a question. Capt. Santosh Chitnis — captain on a ship who loved tennis like a child — was finishing his break and about to head back to duty. At NCL Tennis Courts, he turned to Sudhir: 'Can you arrange a get-together? We'll order sandwiches, play some long tennis together.' Sudhir said yes without knowing exactly what that meant.

What followed was brainstorming with friends at Mhalunge Balewadi — Arjun came up with the round-robin group format that would define Tennisnuts Socials for years. Everyone gets to play equal amounts of tennis, no early knockouts, no one waiting on the sidelines. Simple. Fair. Social.

"Can you arrange a get-together? We'll order some sandwiches and have a long time together."

— Capt. Santosh Chitnis, the man who started it all

Three or four hours of non-stop tennis later, something had quietly started. Nitin Kirtane Sir appreciated what was happening. A community had its first gathering. What came next — hundreds of players, dozens of editions, tournaments, partnerships — none of that was planned. It just grew from that first afternoon.

Thanks to Capt. Santosh Chitnis for the ask that started it all. To Arjun for the format that made it work. To Nitin Kirtane Sir for the early encouragement. And to everyone who showed up for the first one, not knowing what it would become.
