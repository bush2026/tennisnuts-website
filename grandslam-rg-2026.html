How It Played Out

A Two-Week
Chase on Clay

Before each day's play at Roland Garros 2026, Tennisnuts members filled in their predictions for that day's matches — a fresh form every matchday, picks locked in before the first ball, round by round across the Paris fortnight.

After Week 1

Manoj Sharma led the field with 325 points — a 55-point lead over joint second. Shailesh Arun Mistry and Shishir Sanjay Singh shared second on 270, with Usman Nallamandu (265) and Neha (260) close behind.

In 7th, Sudhir Pisal sat at 245 points — watching from 80 back, but with the quarterfinals, semis, and final still to come.

"Sudhir had the best Week 2 of anyone — 475 points. But Manoj's Week 1 lead was 25 more than he could overcome."

The Week 2 Charge

Sudhir Pisal was the story of the second half. He scored 475 points — the best of any participant in Week 2 — across the quarters, semis, and final. Despite that extraordinary charge, Manoj added 420 of his own — third-best in the field — to close the tournament at 745 points and hold on by 25.

Rohit Agarwal surged from 10th to 4th on the back of 430 Week 2 points. Santosh Chitnis went from 14th at half-time to 10th overall, adding 345 in Week 2. Jitendra posted zero in Week 1 but scored 385 in the second half to finish joint 13th. Shailesh Mistry, joint-second after Week 1, had only 270 in Week 2 and fell to 6th.

The Final Margin

Manoj Sharma — 745 pts. Sudhir Pisal — 720. Shishir Sanjay Singh — 660. The Slam Caller was decided by 25 points, confirmed only on the last day of the tournament.

More than thirty members put their tennis minds on the line across the Paris fortnight. The leaderboard stayed live all the way to the final. Manoj Sharma is the Roland Garros 2026 Slam Caller. Wimbledon is next.
