How It Played Out

Roland Garros 2026 — Week 2, Day 14. The clay season's final chapter was already written on the courts of Paris. For 28 Tennisnuts members tracking every match, it came down to who had read the draw best across the full fortnight.

"Only 1 entry per person. Predict wisely. Who will lift the trophy in Paris?"

The predictions game ran across both weeks of the tournament. Members used ATP and WTA rankings as their guide and submitted a single set of predictions — no changes allowed. Points accumulated round by round as results came in from the red clay. By the final, the leaderboard had taken shape and the gaps between positions were clear.

Manoj Sharma topped the final standings with 745 points — a dominant run that placed him comfortably ahead of the field. Sudhir Pisal held second place at 720 points, just 25 behind the leader but unable to close the gap. Shishir Sanjay Singh rounded out the podium at 660. Together the top three separated themselves clearly from the rest of a competitive 28-strong field.

From Neha at 8th to the Baramati-connecting players further down the list — every participant brought their tennis knowledge to the table. The game lived up to its promise: fun, competitive, and a great reason to stay glued to every match from Paris.
