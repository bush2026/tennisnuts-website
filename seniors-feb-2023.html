The Story

Tournament Recap

An overwhelming response from senior players across the city set the tone from the very start. The PMDTA Tennisnuts Tour Feb '23 — the first seniors edition to include both Singles and Doubles categories — was held across two venues: AWTA NCL and CPR, Pune. Clay courts, quality tennis, and the kind of atmosphere that only the seniors circuit seems to produce.

"The match between Sheeladitya and Dr Ravi Kothari was bliss to watch — a semifinal that went to a tiebreak, with amazing points played between two warriors."

The Singles draw produced the tournament's most talked-about storyline: a final between brothers. Dr Rahul Kothari and Dr Ravi Kothari — two outstanding players, one extraordinary family rivalry. Dr Ravi was the undisputed Iron Man of the tournament, playing with extraordinary fitness and gutsy intensity throughout, including a sensational semifinal tiebreak against Sheeladitya that had everyone watching. But in the final, Dr Rahul was the better player on the day, taking the title in what the crowd agreed was a beautiful match to witness.

The Doubles draw told an equally compelling story. Sandeep Belludi and Joy Banerjee were simply outstanding — Sandeep's hands at the net were called the best seen at the tournament, while Joy's serve was a weapon all week. Together they were a complete pair, and their title was fully deserved. Amit Kindo and Sunil Lulla pushed them hard; their combination and understanding of each other's game made them formidable right through the draw.

Other names that made an impression: the Baramati pair of Shashank Mane Sir and Dr Avinash, who made the journey to Pune and played wonderful tennis; Ganesh's serving alongside Joy; and Tennisnuts Panchayat's own players competing with full dedication for the love of the game.

Heartfelt thanks to Venky for ground-0 operations and Amit Kindo for the challenging task of scheduling. To Aniket Wakankar Sir, Nilesh Sir, and Gopal Sir for keeping the courts in excellent condition — a fine clay experience throughout. To Kumar for stepping in to help run CPR on Saturday's second half. And to Chetan and team for their sincerity on court maintenance and watering. Thanks also to Abhishek Tamhane Sir and Himanshu Gosavi Sir of PMDTA for their constant guidance and support.
Categories for 2023

Following the success of this edition, the PMDTA Tennisnuts Tour officially announced four categories for the 2023 seniors circuit:

35+  ·  45+  ·  55+  ·  Open Seniors (15+) — all pros, coaches, and community players welcome.

Match Moments

Players of Note

⚔️
Kothari Brothers Final

When the Singles draw produced a Kothari vs Kothari final, everyone stopped. Dr Rahul and Dr Ravi — identical baseline pedigree, different game plans. Dr Rahul edged it, but the match was a spectacle from start to finish.

⚔️ Family Rivalry
🔥
Sheeladitya vs Dr Ravi

The semifinal that stole the show. Both players raised their level with every point — it went to a tiebreak, and the quality of tennis had everyone watching in silence. A defining match of the tournament.

🔥 Semifinal Thriller
🤲
Sandeep Belludi

Described in the post-tournament notes as having "incredible hands" — Sandeep's net game was the talk of the Doubles draw. Quick, decisive, and able to change the point with a single touch.

🤲 Best Hands
🏡
Baramati Pair

Shashank Mane Sir and Dr Avinash made the trip from Baramati to Pune and played beautiful tennis — a welcome addition to the field and a reminder that Pune's seniors tennis goes well beyond the city limits.

Dr Ravi Kothari

The Iron Man of the tournament. His fitness level throughout — across Singles and a tough bracket — drew universal admiration. Played every point like it was the last and never gave an easy game.
